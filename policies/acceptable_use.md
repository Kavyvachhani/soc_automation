# Acceptable Use Policy
**Attest Corporation — Effective: 2026-01-01 | Version: 1.5 | Owner: IT & Compliance**

---

## 1. Purpose

This policy defines acceptable and prohibited uses of Attest Corporation's technology
resources, including computing devices, networks, software, and data systems, to protect
the Company and its employees from harm.

---

## 2. Scope

Applies to all Company-owned or managed devices, networks, SaaS subscriptions, and cloud
services, used by employees, contractors, and temporary staff.

---

## 3. Acceptable Uses

The following uses are explicitly permitted:

- Performing assigned job duties.
- Reasonable personal use that does not consume excessive bandwidth or interfere with work.
- Using Company-approved communication tools (Slack, email, video conferencing).
- Accessing public internet resources for legitimate work research.
- Participating in approved open-source projects (see Open Source Policy addendum).

---

## 4. Prohibited Uses

The following are **strictly prohibited**:

### 4.1 Security Violations
- Attempting to bypass authentication controls or firewalls.
- Installing or executing malware, spyware, or unauthorized scripts.
- Intercepting or monitoring network traffic without written authorization.
- Storing, transmitting, or executing unlicensed software.
- Sharing credentials or allowing others to use your accounts.

### 4.2 Data Violations
- Exfiltrating Company or customer data to personal devices or storage.
- Uploading Confidential or Restricted data to unapproved cloud services.
- Accessing data beyond your authorized scope (even if technically accessible).
- Disabling, bypassing, or interfering with audit logging.

### 4.3 Content Violations
- Accessing, storing, or transmitting illegal content.
- Harassment, discrimination, or bullying via any Company channel.
- Defaming the Company, its employees, customers, or partners on any platform.
- Cryptocurrency mining using Company resources.

### 4.4 Resource Abuse
- Using Company resources for personal commercial gain.
- Sustained high-bandwidth personal activities (streaming, gaming) during work hours.
- Circumventing the Company's VPN or network monitoring tools.

---

## 5. BYOD (Bring Your Own Device)

Personal devices accessing Company systems must:
- Have up-to-date operating system and security patches installed.
- Have full-disk encryption enabled.
- Have the Company's MDM profile installed (required for email and VPN access).
- Be subject to remote wipe in the event of loss or termination.

---

## 6. Monitoring

Employees have no expectation of privacy when using Company systems. The Company
reserves the right to monitor, audit, and retain all activity on its systems. Monitoring
is conducted in accordance with applicable law and Company privacy notices.

---

## 7. Reporting Violations

Any suspected violation must be reported to security@attest.io. Retaliation against
good-faith reporters is prohibited and will be subject to disciplinary action.

---

## 8. Consequences of Violation

Violations may result in:
- Revocation of technology access.
- Disciplinary action up to and including termination.
- Civil or criminal prosecution where applicable.

---

## 9. Acknowledgement

All employees must acknowledge this policy during onboarding and annually thereafter.
The acknowledgement is logged as part of the Company's SOC 2 evidence collection.
